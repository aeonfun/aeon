## BLUF

Polymarket's Tamil Nadu DMK contract has rerated from 78¢ on March 26 to 86¢ today as exit polls landed, vindicating the 04-27 "DMK 80% mispriced" flag. The residual calibration edge has migrated to the TVK 8.5¢ contract, which exit-poll consensus from eight of nine agencies values closer to 2–4¢. The May 4 count resolves a $19.9M market whose remaining pricing tension is driven entirely by one outlier (Axis My India) with documented demographic-weighting flaws.

## Thesis

**DMK will win the most seats in the 234-seat Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly on May 4, 2026, with >92% probability, making the TVK contract the asymmetric calibration trade — fair value 2–4¢ vs. market 8.5¢, a 4–6¢ edge per share.** Eight of nine named exit-poll agencies project DMK seat ranges of 92–160 with TVK at 10–30 ([thenewsminute.com](https://www.thenewsminute.com/tamil-nadu/all-exit-polls-show-dmk-lead-axis-my-india-says-tvk-to-win-minimum-98-seats)); the lone outlier (Axis My India: TVK 98–120) survives only by a sampling base that misses 31% of the electorate ([oneindia.com](https://www.oneindia.com/election/states/tamilnadu/numbers-that-dont-add-up-reading-tamil-nadus-2026-exit-polls-8073587.html)). At 86¢, the DMK leg is roughly fair-priced; the TVK leg at 8.5¢ is where the remaining calibration gap lives.

## Context

Polling closed April 23 with 85.14% turnout — 12pp above 2021 and the highest ever in any Indian state assembly election ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Tamil_Nadu_Legislative_Assembly_election)). Three-way contest: DMK-led Secular Progressive Alliance (Stalin, incumbent), AIADMK with BJP/PMK, and actor Vijay's Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam (TVK) making a debut run.

Counting is May 4. The Polymarket "Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly Election Winner" market has $19.9M volume and currently prices DMK 86%, TVK 8.5%, AIADMK 7.1% ([polymarket.com](https://polymarket.com/event/tamil-nadu-legislative-assembly-election-winner)). DMK's price moved 78% (Mar 26, $166k vol then per [Asianet](https://newsable.asianetnews.com/india/polymarket-predicts-78-chance-of-dmk-win-in-2026-tamil-nadu-polls-articleshow-uphs8vb)) → ~80% (Apr 27, internal log) → 86% (post-exit-poll, Apr 30) — a 6¢ rerate in three days as the consensus signal landed. The 04-27 flag of DMK underpricing has been ratified directionally; the locus of mispricing has moved to TVK.

## Evidence

- Eight of nine named exit polls (Today's Chanakya, P-Marq, People's Pulse, Matrize, Political Laboratory, Axis My India, JVC, Matrize) project DMK winning 92–160 seats; only JVC dissents with AIADMK 128–147 vs DMK 75–96 ([thenewsminute.com](https://www.thenewsminute.com/tamil-nadu/all-exit-polls-show-dmk-lead-axis-my-india-says-tvk-to-win-minimum-98-seats)).
- TVK seat ranges across all nine agencies: 10–12 (Matrize), 13–18 (Today's Chanakya), 16–26 (P-Marq), 18–24 (People's Pulse), 8–15 (JVC), 10–14 (Political Laboratory) — only Axis My India projects 98–120 ([thenewsminute.com](https://www.thenewsminute.com/tamil-nadu/all-exit-polls-show-dmk-lead-axis-my-india-says-tvk-to-win-minimum-98-seats)).
- Axis My India's 44,460-interview sample over-represents Brahmin voters 3× (9% vs <3% actual) and halves OBC/MBC representation (28% vs 51% actual), with 31% of the electorate unsurveyed — the article concludes "you cannot claim a party has 35% vote share when your survey ignores nearly one-third of the electorate" ([oneindia.com](https://www.oneindia.com/election/states/tamilnadu/numbers-that-dont-add-up-reading-tamil-nadus-2026-exit-polls-8073587.html)).
- 2021 Tamil Nadu exit polls were *directionally* correct for DMK as winner but overstated the margin: agencies projected 175–195 seats; actual was 159 ([oneindia.com](https://www.oneindia.com/election/states/tamilnadu/numbers-that-dont-add-up-reading-tamil-nadus-2026-exit-polls-8073587.html)). The bias direction (overstating DMK) is the *opposite* of what would hurt the Polymarket DMK leg today.
- Debut parties under FPTP face geographic-concentration risk: TVK at 20–25% vote share spread uniformly across 234 constituencies "could produce near-zero seats" ([oneindia.com](https://www.oneindia.com/election/states/tamilnadu/numbers-that-dont-add-up-reading-tamil-nadus-2026-exit-polls-8073587.html)).

## Key papers

These three are the standing prior for trusting an aggregated $19.9M-volume market over any single exit-poll agency. Sourced from OpenAlex search ranking by citations and topical relevance; cited here for framing only — paper PDFs were not deep-read for this brief.

- **Graefe, A. (2014). "Accuracy of Vote Expectation Surveys in Forecasting Elections."** *Public Opinion Quarterly* 78(S1):204-232 — comparative accuracy of vote-expectation surveys vs intent-based polling. 115 citations. https://doi.org/10.1093/poq/nfu008
- **Erikson, R. S. & Wlezien, C. (2012). "Markets vs. polls as election predictors: An historical assessment."** *Electoral Studies* 31(3) — historical comparison of prediction markets and polls as election forecasts. 58 citations. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.electstud.2012.04.008
- **Rothschild, D. (2009). "Forecasting Elections: Comparing Prediction Markets, Polls, and Their Biases."** *Public Opinion Quarterly* 73(5):895-916 — bias-correction methodology for prediction-market vs poll forecasts. 106 citations. https://doi.org/10.1093/poq/nfp082

## What would change my mind

- **JVC poll directionally validated by Stage 1 counting trends** (AIADMK leads by counting hour 2 across >120 of 234 constituencies). Would invalidate the 8/9-consensus dominance argument and force a sharp DMK price collapse.
- **Polymarket TVK contract trades above 15¢** before counting starts. That would either signal late whale flow with information not in exit polls, or large-block manipulation similar to the 04-27 wallet-flow callouts — both warrant pause before betting NO.
- **Axis My India's TVK 98–120 projection** turns out within 20% of actual seats (i.e., TVK ≥78 seats). Would force a full rebuild of the demographic-base critique and recalibrate prior on outlier exit polls broadly.
- **Total turnout-driven youth surge** materializes in counting (booth-level age skew evident in early-trend reports). Would partially rehabilitate the Axis differential-candor concern.

## Open questions

- TVK seat geography: Axis's implied 35% TVK vote share (if even close) cannibalizes DMK or AIADMK depending on Chennai-metro vs Cauvery-delta concentration.
- 12pp turnout jump has no historical model — agencies treat it as Stalin- vs Vijay-mobilization on faith. Counting-day age/region splits are the first hard signal.
- UMA resolution risk: market was scheduled to end April 23 but is still trading pending ECI outcome ([polymarket.com](https://polymarket.com/event/tamil-nadu-legislative-assembly-election-winner)) — parallel to the Iran-cf/Hez-cf UMA basis already tracked.
