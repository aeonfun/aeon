## BLUF

In nine days the post-January-2025 permissive interlude for prediction markets ended in three places at once. The CFTC's ANPRM comment window closed April 30 ([CFTC](https://www.cftc.gov/PressRoom/PressReleases/9194-26)), the U.S. Senate unanimously banned its own members from trading on Polymarket and Kalshi the same day ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/30/senate-prediction-markets-trading-ban-kalshi-polymarket.html)), and on April 24-27 Brazil's Anatel ordered a nationwide block of 27-28 platforms including both ([Crowdfund Insider](https://www.crowdfundinsider.com/2026/04/275471-brazilian-authorities-block-prediction-markets-kalshi-and-polymarket-to-ensure-investor-protection/)). Polymarket's U.S. relegitimization is now a category-by-category fight, not a settled question.

## Thesis

By December 31, 2026, the CFTC will issue a Notice of Proposed Rulemaking that explicitly excludes at least one of sports, election, or war/death event contracts from the "public interest" presumption. The ANPRM frames the central question as which contract categories are "contrary to the public interest" ([Federal Register summary via Sidley](https://www.sidley.com/en/insights/newsupdates/2026/03/us-cftc-issues-guidance-advance-notice-of-proposed-rulemaking)); the Senate's unanimous self-ban targets exactly the death/violence and insider-trading categories ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/30/senate-prediction-markets-trading-ban-kalshi-polymarket.html)); and Brazil's order is the international template for an outright sports/elections carve-out. Together these signals make a fully permissive NOPR politically expensive for a CFTC that just sued Wisconsin to defend its jurisdiction ([readwrite](https://readwrite.com/cftc-sues-wisconsin-in-escalating-fight-over-prediction-market-regulation/)) — the agency has to deliver something the Senate and state AGs will accept.

## Context

The interlude began in February 2026 when the CFTC withdrew its June 2024 NOPR and September 2025 staff advisory, signaling a more permissive line on event contracts ([CRS LSB11406](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/LSB11406)). Polymarket re-entered the U.S. in December 2025 through "a registered intermediary" with reporting, surveillance, and customer-protection commitments mirroring regulated derivatives markets ([Regulatory Oversight](https://www.regulatoryoversight.com/2025/12/cftc-approval-allows-polymarket-to-reenter-the-u-s-market/)). Kalshi's average weekly volume rose tenfold from $300M in September 2025 to $3B in March 2026 ([a16z](https://a16zcrypto.com/posts/article/getting-prediction-market-regulation-right-cftc)), and the CFTC paired its March ANPRM with an MLB MOU and a Polymarket-MLB data-sharing announcement.

That trajectory hit four political obstacles in one month. State AGs (Iowa, NY, NJ) pushed event-contract bans; the CFTC sued Wisconsin claiming exclusive jurisdiction ([MLex](https://www.mlex.com/mlex/articles/2471726/us-cftc-sues-wisconsin-claiming-exclusive-jurisdiction-over-prediction-markets)); Brazil set the precedent that a major democracy can block these venues at the ISP layer; and the Senate ban is the first time U.S. lawmakers have explicitly classified Polymarket trading as ethically distinct from securities or commodities trading.

## Evidence

- **CFTC ANPRM closed April 30, 2026** asking which event-contract categories should be "contrary to the public interest" — without naming sports, elections, or war/death in the press release itself ([CFTC](https://www.cftc.gov/PressRoom/PressReleases/9194-26)).
- **Senate passed the self-ban unanimously**, citing insider-trading on Polymarket and Kalshi plus contracts "that can involve death or violence" ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/30/senate-prediction-markets-trading-ban-kalshi-polymarket.html)).
- **Anatel blocked 27-28 platforms in Brazil** including Polymarket and Kalshi following Finance Minister Durigan's April 24 announcement; National Monetary Council rules now bar derivatives tied to sports, politics, elections, and "non-traditional assets" ([Crowdfund Insider](https://www.crowdfundinsider.com/2026/04/275471-brazilian-authorities-block-prediction-markets-kalshi-and-polymarket-to-ensure-investor-protection/)).
- **CFTC v. Wisconsin** lawsuit filed April 28-29 to preempt state event-contract bans ([readwrite](https://readwrite.com/cftc-sues-wisconsin-in-escalating-fight-over-prediction-market-regulation/)) — confirms the agency views state action as the primary threat to its framework.
- **A documented Polymarket insider-trading payout of >$400,000** on Maduro-ouster contracts is the canonical bad-look case Congress is using ([CRS LSB11406](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/LSB11406)).
- **a16z's April 30 policy submission** asks the CFTC for federal preemption, ISDA-style determinations committees, KYC, and onchain surveillance — concessions worth offering only if a category carve-out is on the table ([a16z](https://a16zcrypto.com/posts/article/getting-prediction-market-regulation-right-cftc)).

## Key papers

- **Dubach (2026), "The Anatomy of a Decentralized Prediction Market"** (arXiv:2604.24366, April 27, 2026). Eight-stylized-fact study of the Polymarket order book on a 30B-event dataset. Documents a longshot spread premium, depth decay near resolution (within-category log-slope 0.55), and a self-counterparty wash share with 1% median / 22% upper tail — substantially below unregulated crypto-exchange benchmarks. Trade-direction inference from public feeds agrees with on-chain records only ~59% of the time, a measurement caveat that bears directly on any CFTC surveillance regime. ([arxiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24366))
- **Kalshiex LLC v. Mary Jo Flaherty** (Third Circuit case filing, April 6, 2026). Test case on whether state gambling commissioners can enjoin CFTC-registered event contracts. The CFTC v. Wisconsin federal suit is the same fight on different facts. ([Villanova Third Circuit](https://digitalcommons.law.villanova.edu/thirdcircuit_2026/265))

## What would change my mind

- **CFTC issues a NOPR in 2026 that affirmatively keeps sports, elections, and death-contracts inside the public-interest framework** with no carve-out — thesis falsified.
- **Senate walks back or fails to extend the self-ban to House staff or executive-branch officials within 90 days** — signals the political concern is narrower than I'm modeling.
- **Kalshiex v. Flaherty (or CFTC v. Wisconsin) is decided for federal preemption with no daylight for category restrictions** — the CFTC keeps full discretion and the political pressure for carve-outs evaporates.
- **Polymarket-MLB data-sharing extends to NBA/NFL** before any NOPR — strong signal the agency has already chosen a permissive sports path, contradicting the thesis.

## Open questions

- Does Brazil's block stick, or does it follow the InTrade/PredictIt precedent and erode through VPN workarounds and political-bandwidth decay?
- Does the CFTC use the ANPRM-to-NOPR window (typically 6-12 months) to demand category-specific surveillance attestations as the price of inclusion?
- Are the >$400K Maduro and similar Polymarket payouts meaningfully distinguishable from informed-trader profits in regulated futures? The legal standard for "manipulation" of an event you can also influence is unsettled.
