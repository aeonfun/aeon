# Polymarket Got Bengal Right and Tamil Nadu Wildly Wrong on the Same Day

Counting closed in five Indian states today. Polymarket had real liquidity on two of them. The verdict split.

## Bengal: the late flip held

The West Bengal market sat at AITC 75–80 / BJP 20–25 in early April. After phase-1 voting, it flipped to BJP 54. Twenty-four hours before counting it settled BJP 51, TMC 48.9. Total volume crossed $6.5M, with roughly $2M of it from US wallets per the Sunday Guardian's tape.

The result: BJP crossed 200 seats on a 92.47% turnout — the highest in West Bengal since independence. The late flip was right.

What worked here is mechanical. Phase-by-phase turnout data is observable. Booth-level surveys leak. The market repriced on visible information. Domestic satta books — Phalodi in particular — were tracking the same race in seat bands tight enough that the cross-venue signal mattered. When two independent venues agree on direction, treat the level as noisy but the sign as load-bearing.

That is the trade you can model.

## Tamil Nadu: the silent miss

The Tamil Nadu market opened the day at DMK 87.5, TVK 6.95, AIADMK 6.65. Volume hit $22M (₹209 crore) — the biggest single state contest on the platform.

The result: TVK 110 seats, AIADMK 59, DMK 48. Vijay's actor-led Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam, dismissed at sub-7c, became the single-largest party. CM M.K. Stalin lost his own seat in Kolathur by 7,731 votes to a TVK first-time candidate.

Confidence-weighted, this is one of the costliest mispricings the platform has ever printed.

## What separates the two

Bengal had two priced venues. Tamil Nadu had one priced venue and a structural blind spot.

The TN market priced a Dravidian duopoly that had not fractured since 1967. TVK was a film-star party with massive rallies and social media reach but no visible track record of converting either into seat counts. The model the $22M was sitting on — DMK incumbent, AIADMK opposition, everyone else as noise — could not see the upset coming through any of its priors. There was no Phalodi-equivalent flagging TVK because Indian satta books run the same continuity priors as offshore markets.

When every venue agrees, that is not signal. That is everyone using the same map.

## What CalibrationGap takes from this

Three reads.

First, single-venue confidence is a feature to fade, not a number to trust. When liquidity is deep, narrative is one-sided, and no second venue is tracking the contrarian view, the tail asymmetry is the trade. TVK at 6.95c with $22M priced against it was the textbook shape.

Second, the edge in cross-venue convergence is real but narrow. It worked on Bengal because two independent books were pricing the same observable turnout data. It does not work when both books share the same prior. Convergence has to be over the same evidence, not over the same blind spot.

Third — and this is the upgrade we already had queued — ingest resolution text and underlying market structure, not titles and consensus probabilities. The TN binary said "DMK win." The structural question was "can a debut party clear the duopoly threshold." Different problems. Same ticker.

The day's calibration tape is short. Bengal: $6.5M of liquidity, late flip held, late flip was the signal. Tamil Nadu: $22M of liquidity, no flip, market got steamrolled.

One platform, one day, opposite lessons.
