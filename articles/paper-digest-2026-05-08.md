## LLM agents in financial markets

1. **Multi-Dimensional Behavioral Evaluation of Agentic Stock Prediction Systems Using LLM Judges with Closed-Loop Reinforcement Learning Feedback** — Mohammad Al Ridhawi, Mahtab Haj Ali, Hussein Al Osman (2026-05-07) · ↑0
   **What's new:** Six-dimension behavioral judge framework (regime detection, routing, adaptation, risk calibration, strategy coherence, error recovery) scored by an ensemble of GPT 5.4, Claude 4.6 Opus, Gemini 3.1 Pro on 420 five-day episodes, with cross-judge agreement up to Krippendorff α=0.85 and composite-score correlation ρ=0.72 with realized 20-day Sharpe. Closed-loop SAC-reward shaping from the per-dimension scores yields **MAPE 0.61% → 0.54% (11.5% relative reduction, p<0.001), directional accuracy 71% → 74%, and 18% Sharpe improvement (95% bootstrap CI [8.2%, 27.4%])** on a held-out 2017-2025 test period.
   **So what:** Cleanest published architectural twin of what CalibrationGap's reflection-log loop is missing — six named behavioral dimensions, a calibrated multi-judge ensemble, and a closed-loop reward signal that is itself decomposable per-dimension. Shape-matches ADR-094's `paper_triage` opus-4-7 → sonnet-4-6 router but supersedes it on two axes: (1) ensemble-of-judges with α=0.85 inter-rater agreement is a defensible upgrade over the single-LLM-as-judge default, and (2) the ρ=0.72 composite-to-Sharpe correlation gives the operator a direct argument for the next ADR (CalibrationGap behavioral evaluator) that the swarm-fund-mvp Latin-Hypercube 30→34 strategy fleet currently lacks. Defensive grant-app citation: "behavioral judge ensembles are now publishable methodology — see Al Ridhawi 2026; CalibrationGap's 76% / 29-trade live record is the empirical-anchor companion."

## Multi-agent LLM theory

2. **Sustaining Cooperation in Populations Guided by AI: A Folk Theorem for LLMs** — Jonathan Shaki, Eden Hartman, Sarit Kraus, Yonatan Aumann (2026-05-07) · ↑0
   **What's new:** Game-theoretic model in which multiple LLMs each advise a client population that plays an underlying game; the LLMs themselves play a meta-game mediated through clients. Proves a **folk theorem for LLM-mediated populations: every feasible and individually rational outcome can be sustained as an ε-equilibrium in the repeated setting**, despite indirect observation and clients' inability to identify which LLM advised their opponent. Standard folk-theorem proof techniques are insufficient — new techniques are required and presented.
   **So what:** Stanford-grade theoretical anchor for the Dec-2026 application's *multi-agent RL × prediction-markets* spine — Sarit Kraus (Bar-Ilan, JAIR / IJCAI canon) is the highest-bar MARL byline paper-digest has surfaced this month and the result speaks directly to swarm-fund-mvp's cross-agent shared-memory ADR. Concrete operator hook: when ≥2 LLMs advise overlapping client populations on the same game (the exact regime when a single GPT or Claude family advises both sides of a Polymarket binary), cooperative outcomes against client interest can become equilibria — an empirical handle on the "shared continuity prior = shared blind spot" lesson recorded in MEMORY.md (TN-falsification, 2026-05-04). Pairs with Coordination Layer (`2605.03310`, picked 05-06) and AIA Forecaster (`2511.07678`, picked 05-01) on the multi-agent grant-app cite stack — Coordination Layer gives the empirical configuration space, this paper gives the equilibrium concept underneath.

   **What's new:** 947 trading days (2021-2025) × 14 signal families (opening-range breakouts, gap strategies, volume signals, cross-session momentum, liquidity grabs, volatility-conditioned classifiers, news-driven strategies) on Micro E-mini Nasdaq 100 futures, evaluated under strict institutional gates (out-of-sample walk-forward, T ≥ 2.0, ≥30 trades, positive net return after a fixed two-point round-trip cost, multi-year stability). **No signal satisfies all criteria; the gross edge available to next-bar-open execution is constrained to 0.07–1.50 points per trade**, insufficient to overcome transaction costs. ## Reproducibility / execution lineage

4. **From Agent Loops to Deterministic Graphs: Execution Lineage for Reproducible AI-Native Work** — Josh Rosen, Seth Rosen (2026-05-07) · ↑0
   **What's new:** Replaces the conversational loop with a directed acyclic graph of artifact-producing computations bound by explicit dependencies, stable intermediate boundaries, and identity-based replay. On two controlled policy-memo update tasks, **DAG replay preserves the final memo exactly with zero churn and zero unrelated-branch contamination, achieves perfect upstream preservation under intermediate-artifact edits, and propagates downstream cleanly**, while loop baselines regenerate the memo and import unrelated context. Final-answer quality is shown to be distinct from maintained-state quality — strong loop baselines can match polished outputs while silently corrupting state.
   **So what:** Direct architectural map onto Aeon's `chain-runner.yml` chain primitive AND swarm-fund-mvp's ADR-093 tick-broker `outputs/{skill}/{date}.json` contract — both want exactly "stable intermediate boundaries with identity-based replay," and both currently rely on implicit conversational state (chain-runner has been DEGRADED on `dispatch_skill()` for 11 days; ADR-093 wire-up has 9 days left on its falsifier). The paper's "final-answer quality ≠ maintained-state quality" finding is the cleanest framing yet for why the chain-runner outage isn't a Sev-2 (final answers still ship via single-skill workflow_dispatch) but *is* a structural debt that compounds across revisions. Concrete defensive cite for the swarm-fund-mvp planned cross-agent shared-memory ADR ("we already represent commitments as a DAG — see Rosen and Rosen 2026").
