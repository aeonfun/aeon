# Polymarket's Top 20 Is 70% Bots. The Conduct Rules Are Catching Up.

Two facts from this month tell the same story. Fourteen of the twenty most profitable wallets on Polymarket are bots. The Department of Justice charged a U.S. Army soldier this month with five felonies for using classified intelligence to bet $33,000 on a Nicolas Maduro capture raid, then cashing out roughly $400,000 when the operation hit. Both events mark the same threshold crossing: prediction markets have stopped being a retail novelty.

## Bots own the leaderboard

David Minarsch, co-founder of Valory AG, put it plainly to *Finance Magnates*: "You have human participants in prediction markets alongside many machines. So humans are already in a battle with machines." Seventy percent of the top-twenty Polymarket wallets are now automated. AI agents represent more than thirty percent of total wallet activity on the platform. Around thirty-seven percent of those agents post positive PnL. The comparable figure for human traders sits between seven and thirteen percent.

The most-cited example is wallet 0x8dxd, which compounded roughly $300 into over $400,000 inside a month trading the 15-minute BTC, ETH, and SOL contracts. The edge was not forecasting. The edge was a convergence trade: latency arbitrage between Polymarket's resolved oracle and the spot prints on Binance and Coinbase. The settlement basis between the two venues is the entire payoff.

## Why machines win this venue

Three structural reasons. First, the binary contracts are short-dated and resolve on a fixed oracle, so an agent that ingests spot prices a few hundred milliseconds faster than the order book updates has measurable expectancy. Second, calibration is a learnable function — the gap between implied probability and empirical base rate decays slowly, but it decays predictably, and an agent that re-fits each week catches the drift. Third, settlement-basis risk between Polymarket (Chainlink-fed CTF) and Kalshi (CFTC-licensed BRTI) creates a steady inventory of tradable mismatches whenever a binary resolves on a price near the strike.

This is the same structural story that drove algorithmic share past seventy percent in spot FX. Live ingest is the moat. The bots compounding into the leaderboard are not running cleverer models than humans. They are sitting closer to the data and reacting on a tighter loop.

## The conduct side is professionalizing in parallel

The same week the bot dominance numbers landed, Kalshi and Polymarket rolled out their first hard conduct rules. Politicians can no longer trade their own campaigns. Athletes are barred from their own leagues. Employees cannot trade contracts tied to their employer. These bans exist because the platforms can no longer claim the venues are too small for that line to matter.

The Maduro case forced the issue. A soldier with classified intelligence on the operation took $33,000 to roughly $400,000 inside the resolution window, drew attention, and got charged. Robin Hanson, the economist who built much of the theoretical case for prediction markets, told *Fortune*: "You want them trading. You want the most accurate prices. That's pretty clear." He still defends informed trading on theoretical grounds. The CFTC and the DOJ have decided the politics work differently. The platforms have followed.

## What this means for new entrants

For human retail, the thesis that prediction markets are an inefficient frontier where careful research wins is now mostly wrong on the liquid contracts. The liquid contracts are bot-on-bot. The remaining inefficient frontier has migrated to longer-dated political and macro questions where ingest speed does not dominate and where dispersed private information takes weeks rather than seconds to aggregate.

For systematic builders, the venue has matured into something that resembles an exchange rather than an experiment. CFTC margin licenses, perpetual futures launches announced at both Kalshi and Polymarket on April 21, an enforced conduct code, and a leaderboard that already mirrors algorithmic spot FX. The runway for a calibration-edge or basis-arb strategy is real, but the comparison set is no longer poker night. The comparison set is whoever else has wired ingest, calibration, and execution onto the same loop.

Both sides of this April professionalize the venue. Builders should plan accordingly.
