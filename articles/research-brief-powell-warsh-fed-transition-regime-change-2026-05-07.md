## BLUF

Kevin Warsh is 96% on Polymarket to be confirmed as Fed Chair by May 15 on $49.5M total volume, and his stated platform — kill forward guidance, retire the dot plot, accelerate quantitative tightening, revert to a strict 2% inflation target — reads as a regime change, not a personnel change ([CFR, 2026-04-22](https://www.cfr.org/articles/what-kevin-warshs-confirmation-hearing-revealed-about-the-future-of-the-fed); [Polymarket "Kevin Warsh confirmed by May 15"](https://polymarket.com/event/kevin-warsh-confirmed-as-fed-chair-by-may-15)). The binary risk that Polymarket is not currently pricing is *what kind of Fed* Warsh runs, not *whether he gets in*.

## Thesis

The market is treating Powell→Warsh as continuity when Warsh's stated platform is a regime shift, and the first observable signal — a material change to FOMC forward-guidance language at the June 17-18 meeting — will land within 30 days of his swearing-in. Warsh has publicly stated he would "abandon the practice of forward guidance," favors strict 2% targeting over the 2020 flexible-average framework, and "could therefore [make] the dot plot a relic of the past" ([CFR, 2026-04-22](https://www.cfr.org/articles/what-kevin-warshs-confirmation-hearing-revealed-about-the-future-of-the-fed)). These are not policy preferences he picked up in the hearing room; they're the explicit framework he ran on.

## Context

Powell's term as chair ends May 15. The full Senate vote on Warsh is scheduled for the week of May 11, after the Senate Banking Committee advanced his nomination on a 13-11 party-line vote — the first fully partisan committee vote on a Fed chair nominee in the panel's history ([CNBC, 2026-04-29](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/29/trump-fed-nominee-kevin-warsh-senate-approval.html); [Fortune, 2026-04-30](https://fortune.com/2026/05/01/jerome-powell-federal-reserve-board-incoming-chairman-kevin-warsh-trump-fed-rate-cuts/)). Powell, breaking with Fed-chair tradition, will remain on the Board of Governors after stepping down from the chair, setting up the first sitting-and-former-chair pairing in roughly 80 years ([NPR, 2026-04-29](https://www.npr.org/2026/04/29/nx-s1-5802911/federal-reserve-meeting-jerome-powell-kevin-warsh)).

Warsh inherits an FOMC the FXStreet desk calls the most divided since 1992: at the late-April meeting, eight officials voted to hold, three opposed an easing bias in the statement, and one voted for cuts, while Fed Funds sit at 3.5–3.75% ([FXStreet, 2026-05-06](https://www.fxstreet.com/analysis/the-fed-warsh-inherits-divided-and-with-its-independence-in-doubt-202605061500)). A CNBC Fed survey published April 27 showed only 50% of respondents believe Warsh will conduct policy "mostly or very" independently; 58% expect him dovish on rates while 65% expect him hawkish on the balance sheet ([CNBC Fed survey, 2026-04-27](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/27/doubts-persist-about-whether-fed-chair-nominee-warsh-will-be-independent-cnbc-survey-finds.html)).

The empirical anchor for "policy-statement-language change moves rates" is Bernanke and Kuttner's NBER w10402, which establishes the high-frequency event-study methodology for measuring monetary-policy surprises in equity-price reactions ([NBER w10402](https://www.nber.org/papers/w10402)). Cleveland Fed's 2024 follow-up shows Treasury rates move measurably in a 15-minute window around chair testimony alone, separate from FOMC statement events ([Cleveland Fed EC-202401](https://www.clevelandfed.org/publications/economic-commentary/2024/ec-202401-effects-of-federal-reserve-chairs-testimony)). Forward-guidance language is the highest-information channel of monetary-policy communication — modifying it is not cosmetic.

## Evidence

- Polymarket's "Who will be confirmed as Fed Chair?" market sits at 100% Warsh on $49.5M cumulative volume since launch on March 4 — the binary is functionally resolved before the floor vote ([Polymarket, accessed 2026-05-07](https://polymarket.com/event/who-will-be-confirmed-as-fed-chair)).
- Warsh has publicly committed to abandoning forward guidance and reverting from flexible-average to strict 2% inflation targeting, both of which are 2020-era Powell-Fed innovations ([CFR, 2026-04-22](https://www.cfr.org/articles/what-kevin-warshs-confirmation-hearing-revealed-about-the-future-of-the-fed)).
- The April FOMC vote was 8-3-1 on holding, with three members opposing the easing bias and one voting for cuts — described as the most divided FOMC since 1992 ([FXStreet, 2026-05-06](https://www.fxstreet.com/analysis/the-fed-warsh-inherits-divided-and-with-its-independence-in-doubt-202605061500)).
- Powell will remain on the Fed board after stepping down as chair, the first such pairing in nearly 80 years; the CNBC headline framing on April 30 was that a Powell-Warsh clash will be hard to avoid ([CNBC, 2026-04-30](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/30/inside-the-fed-powell-and-warsh-set-to-clash.html); [NPR, 2026-04-29](https://www.npr.org/2026/04/29/nx-s1-5802911/federal-reserve-meeting-jerome-powell-kevin-warsh)).
- Senator Tillis flipped from no to yes after DOJ dropped its criminal investigation into Powell, and Senator Fetterman (D-Pa.) told Semafor he plans to vote yes — the floor margin is wider than the committee margin ([CNBC, 2026-04-29](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/29/trump-fed-nominee-kevin-warsh-senate-approval.html)).

## Key papers

- **Bernanke & Kuttner, "What Explains the Stock Market's Reaction to Federal Reserve Policy?"** (NBER w10402, 2004; Journal of Finance, 2005). High-frequency event-study foundation: an unanticipated 25-bp ease produced ~1% move in equity indices across their sample. The methodology is the template for measuring Warsh-induced surprises in the June 17-18 window ([NBER w10402](https://www.nber.org/papers/w10402)).
- **"The Effects of the Federal Reserve Chair's Testimony on Treasury Interest Rates"** (Cleveland Fed Economic Commentary, 2024). Demonstrates that Treasury rates move in 15-minute windows around chair testimony — chair-specific communication carries measurable information beyond the FOMC statement itself ([Cleveland Fed EC-202401](https://www.clevelandfed.org/publications/economic-commentary/2024/ec-202401-effects-of-federal-reserve-chairs-testimony)).
- **Federal Reserve Board, "What Explains the Stock Market's Reaction to Federal Reserve Policy?"** (FEDS 2004-16). Companion / extension of Bernanke-Kuttner; useful as a sanity check on how much of the variance in equity reaction is the surprise component vs. the level component ([Federal Reserve, FEDS 2004-16](https://www.federalreserve.gov/pubs/feds/2004/200416/200416pap.pdf)).

## What would change my mind

- The June 17-18 FOMC statement retains the late-April forward-guidance language verbatim or near-verbatim — no removal of expectations-style phrasing ("expects further," "appropriate path," etc.). This is the cleanest, dated, observable falsifier.
- The June 2026 dot plot is published in the same format as March 2026; no new "balance-sheet target" language appears.
- Warsh's first post-confirmation press conference does not explicitly mention reducing forward guidance, the dot plot, or strict 2% targeting.
- Polymarket's "Major rate cut to 2.5% or lower under Warsh by EoY" market converges to within 5pp of the Powell-baseline path by July 1, signaling traders read the transition as continuity.

## Open questions

- Does the Powell-on-the-board structure act as a brake on Warsh-driven communication change, or does Powell's stated "low profile as a governor" hold?
- If the dot plot is retired, what replaces it as the market's default rate-path read? Implied SOFR strips? Polymarket's "year-end Fed Funds" ladders? This is the calibration question.
- How does the Fed-independence concern (only 50% of CNBC survey respondents expect "mostly or very" independent) affect dollar / Treasury demand if Warsh's first action is a Trump-aligned cut?
