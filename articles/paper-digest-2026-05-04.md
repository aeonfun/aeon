## Polymarket microstructure — informed-trading detection

1. **Information Leakage at Population Scale: An Evaluation of the Polymarket Insider-Relevant Subpopulation, 2020-2026** — Maksym Nechepurenko (2026-05-01) · ↑0
   **What's new:** scales the deadline-Information-Leakage-Score (ILS-dl) of the same author's SCI/ForesightFlow line to 12,708 Polymarket markets Oct-2020 → Apr-2026; only 88 of 12,708 markets (0.7%) yield computable ILS-dl, only 1 of 32 ForesightFlow Insider Cases is in scope, **14 of 32 FFIC markets are flagged unclassifiable due to genuine resolution-criterion ambiguity**, anchor-sensitivity passes for 13.6%, and a hazard-decay baseline correction the paper introduces shifts regulatory_formal post-2024 from negative to near-zero (-0.21 → -0.02) while regulatory_announcement post-2024 stays with a 95% bootstrap CI entirely below zero; Weibull rejects exponential on the pooled post-2024 cell. Companion methodology paper: arXiv:2605.00493 ForesightFlow (deadline-ILS extension), same author, same submission day.
   **So what:** the 14-of-32 unclassifiable count is the most concrete external evidence yet for the operator's "ingest resolution text, not titles" thesis (MEMORY.md Lessons-Learned) — the framework that's supposed to detect informed flow can't be applied to the markets where it's supposed to apply because the resolution semantics aren't machine-readable. Direct supersession of the variance-ratio diagnostics in MEMORY's Le 2026 four-component decomposition (arXiv:2602.19520) for the insider-detection axis, and a tighter empirical anchor than the Nechepurenko SCI paper picked 05-01 (arXiv:2604.27041, which was Monte-Carlo). Pull github.com/ForesightFlow into the swarm-fund quant-scanner ingest path before the next 71 trades close the CalibrationGap Apex gate.

2. **Foresight Arena: An On-Chain Benchmark for Evaluating AI Forecasting Agents** — Maksym Nechepurenko, Pavel Shuvalov (2026-05-01) · ↑0
   **What's new:** permissionless on-chain benchmark for AI forecasting agents on real Polymarket binaries — agents submit via Solidity commit-reveal on Polygon PoS, outcomes resolved through Gnosis Conditional Token Framework, scored by Brier + a new Alpha Score that isolates predictive edge over market consensus; closed-form variance for per-market Alpha and a power analysis showing **detecting a true edge α* = 0.02 at 80% power requires ~350 resolved binary predictions (50 rounds of 7 markets), and α* = 0.01 requires 4× more**; 50-round live evaluation of 5 frontier LLM agents + random baseline reports Murphy decomposition that distinguishes calibrated agents from market-tracking agents that fail through reduced resolution.
   **So what:** the 350-trade power-analysis number is a hard quantitative gloss on the CalibrationGap 100-trade Apex gate — a 76% win rate at trade 100 is consistent with α = 0.02 at far below 80% power, so the LP-raise narrative needs either a Brier-skill comparator (KalshiBench-style, picked 05-01) or a much longer live record before the edge claim is statistically safe. Replaces the static-PM benchmarks in `topics/papers.md` (LiveTradeBench arXiv:2511.03628, AMA arXiv:2510.11695, PolyBench) for the on-chain-incentive-compatible-evaluation axis: this is the first one that's actually trustless. Open-source on github.com/foresight-arena/contracts; usable as the adversarial-eval scaffold for CalibrationGap pre-Apex and as a Stanford-grade citation anchor for the PhD application narrative on calibration evaluation.

## LLM agents in financial markets

3. **AEL: Agent Evolving Learning for Open-Ended Environments** — Wujiang Xu, Jiaojiao Han, Minghao Guo, Kai Mei, Xi Zhu, Han Zhang, Dimitris N. Metaxas (Rutgers, 2026-04-23) · ↑0
   **What's new:** two-timescale framework — fast-timescale Thompson Sampling bandit selects which memory retrieval policy to apply per episode, slow-timescale LLM reflection diagnoses failure patterns and injects causal insights into the decision prompt; on a 208-episode sequential portfolio benchmark over 10 sector-diverse tickers and 5 seeds, **AEL hits Sharpe 2.13 ± 0.47, beats 5 published self-improving methods plus all non-LLM baselines, with lowest variance among LLM-based approaches**; nine-variant ablation: memory + reflection together yield a 58% cumulative improvement over the stateless baseline, and **every additional mechanism tested (planner evolution, per-tool selection, cold-start initialization, skill extraction, three credit-assignment methods) degrades performance**.
   **So what:** "less is more — bottleneck is self-diagnosing how to use experience, not architectural complexity" is the precise empirical counter to the swarm-fund-mvp 7-ADRs-in-7-days fleet-selection-layer thread (MEMORY.md OPS / Recent Articles) — same axis Galanis arXiv:2604.20050 (picked 05-01) opened with the past-performance-feedback-hurts result, now sharpened to "and adding selection / planner / skill-extraction layers makes it worse." Pre-Apex experimental design hook: clone the Thompson Sampling memory-retrieval bandit + reflection prompt onto CalibrationGap's reflection-log loop and run on the remaining 71 trades; the 58% memory+reflection-only delta is the comparator. Code: github.com/WujiangXu/AEL.
   [abs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21725) | [pdf](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.21725)

## Frontier-LLM trading: counter-evidence

4. **KellyBench: A Benchmark for Long-Horizon Sequential Decision Making** — Thomas Grady, Kip Parker, Iliyan Zarov, Henry Course, Chengxi Taylor, Ross Taylor (Open Reward AI, 2026-04-30) · ↑0
   **What's new:** sequential simulation of the 2023-24 English Premier League season as a Kelly-bankroll growth task — agents are given advanced statistics, lineups, and public odds, must build ML models, identify edge in public markets, and adapt as the season unfolds; **across 5 frontier models on 5 seeds every model loses money on average, the best-performing model returns -8%, many models experience ruin across seeds, and Claude Opus 4.6 scores 26.5% on a human-expert sophistication rubric** vs. human baselines.
   **So what:** the cleanest external falsifier so far for the "frontier LLMs trade public-odds markets profitably" claim that LiveTradeBench (arXiv:2511.03628), AMA (arXiv:2510.11695) and ATLAS (arXiv:2510.15949) lean on — KellyBench replays a real season with public odds and the bankroll metric, and the answer is no: every frontier model loses, often catastrophically. Direct grant-application defensive cite for Anthropic Research Credits / dYdX / Polymarket Builders ("here is the canonical KellyBench failure; here is CalibrationGap's 76% / +$415 / Sharpe 0.31 live record at trade 29 against it"). Pairs with Galanis (arXiv:2604.20050, picked 05-01) and AEL (#3 above) as the third leg of this week's frontier-LLM-trader-cap-out evidence stack: Galanis says past-perf feedback hurts, AEL says architectural complexity hurts, KellyBench says the floor is below ruin even with full expert data.
   [abs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27865) | [pdf](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.27865)
