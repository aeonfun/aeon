**Rule:** When a headhunter or internal recruiter says the profile "doesn't fit" or lacks "pedigree," treat it as a statement about *their pipeline*, not Thomas's value. Do not update self-assessment downward under social pressure.

**Why:** Apr 15 call with Jaia Verner (Albert Bow-style quant recruiter) was the archetype. Her business is placing Optiver/Two Sigma alumni into startups founded by Optiver/Two Sigma alumni. She told Thomas directly: internal recruiters at those shops "won't even bother to contact you" based on profile. That's a pipeline statement, not a talent evaluation. Contrast with Ed's recruiter conversation same week — treating Thomas seriously for GM/leadership roles (Kraken XDocs co-GM, Quantbox DeFi, Blockhouse). Same candidate, different pipelines, opposite signals.

**How to apply:**
- **Audience matters more than pedigree.** For LPs, PnL ends the conversation. For Tower/DRW/D.E. Shaw incubations, PnL is the ticket to the room; they underwrite *process + talent gravity*. For Harmonic-style critics, PnL reframes "is this real" into "what's the methodology" (then Aristotle is the answer).
- **Don't apply through resume-screen layers** for shops that matter. Go founder-to-founder. Thomas already does this well (Coinbase via existing relationships, DRW via Bobby/Cumberland, Tower via ventures team).
- **Talent is the real moat, not pedigree.** Quant researchers follow interesting problems and credible leaders, not money. Swarm Lab's "a research lab, the fund is the experiment" framing is the talent magnet. A Two Sigma alum starting a crypto fund has pedigree and a blank Notion doc; Thomas has the actual problem (cross-market convergence, Polymarket×HL, agentic research loop) and a live shipping system. For the right researcher, Thomas's seat is the more interesting one.
- **PnL is necessary but not sufficient** with sophisticated quant shops. Sharpe over 29 trades is statistical noise to D.E. Shaw. What they buy is the research program (5 RQs) + Aristotle methodology + live data moat (Polymarket CLOB + HL execution history).
- **Counter-frame phrasing when a recruiter deflects:** "That sounds like a pipeline constraint on your side, not a fit question. Your clients screen for Optiver/Two Sigma alumni starting firms; I'm building one with a different methodology backed by live P&L and shipped infrastructure. If any of them turn their head toward on-chain markets, keep me in mind." (This is roughly what Thomas said to Jaia — keep this as the template.)

**Do NOT:**
- Argue pedigree ("but I was at Citi, I have CFA"). It reinforces their frame.
- Pitch the fund. Pitch the research program + talent density + problem.
- Treat resume-happy recruiters as signal about strategic direction. They're a distribution channel for a specific product shape Thomas doesn't fit.
