### Idea 1 — Realized (executable)

**Thesis** (1 sentence): Live-ingested, tick-resolution dataset of resolved prediction-market binaries with calibration scoring baked in — the Bloomberg historical backbone for the AI-forecasting and agent-trading stack, not yet-another live-trading unified API.

**ICP** (role + trigger event): Quant researcher or ML engineer at a YC W26 AI-native hedge fund (Modelence's RFS list shows ~5–10 in the batch alone) who just got told to backtest a Polymarket binary strategy and hit the `/prices-history` 12-hour-granularity wall on resolved markets.

**Wedge** (first 12 months): One product — `realized.fyi` — paid REST + Parquet dump of every resolved Polymarket and Kalshi binary since 2024-01-01 at full tick resolution, with Brier / Brier-skill score, regime tag (vol-regime + narrative), and event-class taxonomy attached per market. Free public calibration leaderboard at the same domain pulls developers in.

  - "Accessing reliable data is a challenge — every platform exposes different pieces of information, in different formats, leaving traders without a unified historical view" — https://www.finfeedapi.com/products/prediction-markets-api
  - HN Show HN context (developers describe the data layer as "a mess") — https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46575615

**Monetization**: $300/mo dev tier (REST, last 30 days tick), $1k/mo institutional (full Parquet history + Brier-Skill backtester), $5k/mo data-license (academic / LLM-eval teams). Target gross margin 80%+ — storage + bandwidth heavy not compute-heavy. Unit econ: at $1k MRR per institutional seat, 30 seats = $30k MRR, ~$3k/mo infra.

**Distribution** (specific channel + CAC estimate): Open the calibration leaderboard publicly (every resolved market, Brier-scored, sliced by category and resolution venue) → HN Show HN → AI-forecasting Discord servers (FutureSearch, Manifold, BERI). CAC est. $200-400/dev-tier signup, $1.5k/institutional.

**Moat** (what compounds): Continuous tick ingest from launch day forward — every day not running ingest is an unrecoverable data gap, so latecomers can't catch up. Calibration-tagging schema (regime + event-class) becomes the de-facto research taxonomy if cited in two AI-forecasting papers. Polymarket itself is hostile to fine-grained resolved-market data per issue #216, so Polymarket-as-incumbent-substitute is structurally weak.

**Why now (2026)**: Dome's acquisition by Polymarket in Q1 2026 collapsed the unified-API ecosystem; the surviving live-data competitors (pmxt, Prediction Hunt v2, FinFeedAPI) are all live-stream-shaped, none are calibration-research-shaped. Score top-100 high-volume resolved markets with Brier + Brier-Skill, slice by event class. Stand up calibration.fyi public leaderboard. Post Show HN. Metric: ≥10 paid signups (any tier) within 30 days of launch.

**Expansion** (what if it works): License dataset to LLM-eval teams (OpenAI evals, Anthropic Claude evals, METR) at $10k+/year per consumer. Add Predikt + Limitless + Manifold. ### Idea 2 — Postmortem (ambitious)

**Thesis** (1 sentence): Replay-grade audit chain for autonomous trading agents — open-source instrumentation library plus hosted SaaS that lets a quant fund's compliance officer reconstruct any trade in 30 seconds with the exact features, regime state, sizing logic, and risk-veto path the agent saw, hash-chained for SOX/AI-Act-grade auditor-readiness.

**ICP** (role + trigger event): CTO or Head of Quant at a YC W26 AI-native hedge fund or a CFTC-regulated CTA adding agent strategies, trigger event = first compliance review where the auditor asks "show me why the agent did that trade" and the team has tracebacks but no replayable decision log.

**Wedge** (first 12 months): Python decorator (`@postmortem.trace`) that captures features-at-decision, regime tag, model output, sizing call, risk-veto path, fill — all timestamped, hash-chained, and shipped to a hosted log replay. Replay UI shows "what the agent saw at 14:23:07 UTC and why it sized 0.4%." Single regulator-export button produces a SOX/AI-Act-shaped report.
