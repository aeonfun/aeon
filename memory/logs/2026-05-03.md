## Technical Explainer
- **Topic:** PolySwarm KL/JS divergence engine for prediction-market negation-pair mispricing detection (single mechanism inside arXiv:2604.03888, today's daily-slot paper-pick)
- **Title:** PolySwarm reads two markets as one distribution. The gap is the trade.

### Mechanism articulation (the one move)
Distribution-vs-distribution scoring instead of price-vs-price scoring. CalibrationGap's existing scanner compares the market's price to the swarm's *point estimate* — a scalar gap. PolySwarm compares the market's implied distribution to the swarm's full distribution, gets a JS-divergence number bounded in [0, log 2] ≈ [0, 0.693] nats, and triggers off that. Layered on top: semantic-similarity title matching to surface negation pairs (E vs ¬E), where the no-arbitrage check collapses back to "do the two YES prices sum to 1?" That's the operator's "Iran ceasefire NO 0.25% vs Hez ceasefire YES 99.85%" tradable hook — the mechanism the existing quant scanner is structurally blind to.

### Honesty-forcing finding
The paper publishes formulas (KL eq. 4, JS eq. 5) and gates (EV > 5%, swarm std < 30%) but publishes no result numbers — no Brier, no calibration error, no log-loss, no Sharpe, no win rate, no actual divergence threshold value, no count of markets evaluated. Article surfaces this explicitly in the "What Would Break This" section. Prevents the paper-pick log's "Defensive grant-app citation" framing from inflating into uncritical endorsement.
