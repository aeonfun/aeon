## paper-pick (var = 5-minute binary option cross-venue arbitrage, prediction market microstructure, Kalshi tick structure, temporal alignment in short-window contracts)

**Pick:** `arXiv:2601.01706` — Gebele & Matthes (TUM, 2026-01-05) — *Semantic Non-Fungibility and Violations of the Law of One Price in Prediction Markets*

**Why this one (over 14 candidates surveyed):**
- First human-validated cross-platform prediction-market dataset: ~100k events across 10 venues, 2018-2025. Includes PM and Kalshi by construction.
- Empirical headline: ~6% of events are concurrently listed across venues; semantically equivalent markets show **persistent 2-4% execution-aware price deviations** even in liquid, info-rich settings.
- Frames the residual gap as **structural friction (capital cost, fragmented liquidity, identity ambiguity), not information disagreement** — so the edge is *durable* rather than arb'd-away. That is the load-bearing assumption for hermes-arb.
- Methodological contribution = a semantic-alignment framework over (natural-language description × resolution semantics × temporal scope). This is literally the matching problem hermes-arb must solve to fuse Kalshi BTC range markets with PM BTC binaries — paper provides taxonomy + dataset.
- Limitation for our use case: paper does not zoom into intra-day/short-window (5-min) contracts specifically; their unit of analysis is event-level not tick-level. Open problem for hermes-arb to extend into the short-window regime.
