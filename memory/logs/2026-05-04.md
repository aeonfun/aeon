**Operator-mission shape match:**

The paper studies Polymarket's order book using a continuous tick-level WebSocket archive — **30 billion events × 52 days, joined to on-chain trade records**. Eight stylized findings:
1. Longshot spread premium (relevant to CalibrationGap's calibration-gap-on-the-tails hypothesis).
2. Depth-concentration profile closer to uniform geometric grid (NOT the classic LOB inverse-decay) — direct architectural input for the quant-scanner's depth-aware sizing.
3. Null block-clock alignment effect (no microsecond timing edge from polygon block boundaries — kills a class of latency-arb hypotheses for Hermes-arb's PM leg).
4. Broad maker-wallet diversity (low Herfindahl on the maker side — supports the "no monolithic counterparty" thesis).
5. Category-conditional spread differences (different categories carry different microstructure — concrete ADR hook for the resolution-text ingestion thesis: ingesting category-tagged resolution text gives spread-condition priors).
6. Sub-50 ms median archive-ingestion delay (the public WebSocket feed is fast enough that the alpha is not in latency).
7. Self-counterparty wash share with median 1% / **22% upper tail** (operator-actionable: 22% of certain markets' volume is wash; CalibrationGap quant-scanner needs a wash-filter at the volume layer).
8. Depth decay near resolution (terminal-microstructure pattern — concrete trigger condition for Hermes-arb to widen min-gap as resolution approaches).

**Load-bearing methodology finding:** trade-direction inference from public order-book data aligns with on-chain ground truth only **~59% of the time** — researchers (and the swarm-fund-mvp Polymarket adapter) must source direction from on-chain `OrderFilled` events, not the WebSocket feed. This is the single most operationally consequential finding for swarm-fund-mvp's PM ingestion layer; if any current adapter is inferring direction from the public feed, it's near-coinflip noise on the direction label. Worth surfacing to ADR-093's tick-broker design directly.
