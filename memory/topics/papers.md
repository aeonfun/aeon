- **arXiv:2605.00420** *Foresight Arena: An On-Chain Benchmark for Evaluating AI Forecasting Agents* (Maksym Nechepurenko / Pavel Shuvalov, May 1 2026, cs.MA primary / cs.LG / q-fin.GN, 27pp) — picked 2026-05-04 daily-slot second-invocation. - **arXiv:2604.24366** *The Anatomy of a Decentralized Prediction Market: Microstructure Evidence from the Polymarket Order Book* (Philipp D. Dubach, Apr 27 2026, q-fin.TR / cs.GT / q-fin.GN, 15pp / 9 figs / 4 tables, single-author, no listed affiliation) — picked 2026-05-04 daily slot. Empirical microstructure study of Polymarket from a continuous tick-level WebSocket order-book archive — 30 billion events × 52 days joined to on-chain trade records. Eight stylized findings: longshot spread premium; depth-concentration profile closer to uniform geometric grid (not the classic LOB inverse-decay); null block-clock alignment effect; broad maker-wallet diversity; category-conditional spread differences; sub-50 ms median archive-ingestion delay; self-counterparty wash share with median 1% / 22% upper tail; depth decay near resolution. **Load-bearing methodology finding for swarm-fund-mvp:** trade-direction inference from public order-book data aligns with on-chain ground truth only ~59% of the time — researchers (and the Polymarket adapter) must source direction from on-chain `OrderFilled` events, not the WebSocket feed. Code released: `github.com/philippdubach/polymarket-microstructure`. **Operator-mission shape match:** direct upgrade map for CalibrationGap PM ingestion + Hermes-arb Polymarket leg; the 22%-tail self-counterparty wash and category-conditional spreads are concrete ADR hooks for the resolution-text ingestion thesis (some categories carry venue noise the title-only path can't see). Pairs with already-picked **arXiv:2601.01706** Gebele LOOP (cross-market microstructure violations) and queued **arXiv:2510.15612** SoK DePMs (architectural taxonomy) — completes the Polymarket-microstructure citation trilogy for grant applications (Polymarket Builders Program in particular) and the PhD application's microstructure axis.
