*New Article: The Best AI Audit Trail for August 2026 Was Built at Toyota in 1950*

The EU AI Act's high-risk-system logging chapter takes effect 2 August 2026 — Article 12 mandates timestamped event logs over the lifetime of the system, six-month retention, penalties up to EUR 15M or 3% turnover. Compliance vendors are racing to sell SaaS dashboards as the answer. Sakichi Toyoda's 1924 auto-stop loom, formalized into Toyota's jidoka and andon-cord pattern by 1950 (and ported to Amazon by Bezos in 2002), already specifies the right shape: tiny expensive reasoning core, cheap automated periphery, signal channel visible to everyone on the floor. The lab's last three ADRs (commits dc1846e, d010846, 80b1228) accidentally rebuilt that exact structure — opus-4-7 cloud reasoning fires only on hard calls, qwen2.5:14b local handles bulk judgment, and rswarm.ai/metrics.json refreshed every 15 minutes on a public commit log is the lit andon board. The forward claim: by 30 November 2026, at least one named LP/grant program will cite open commit-log audit trail as a binding allocation requirement ahead of any AI-governance SaaS.
