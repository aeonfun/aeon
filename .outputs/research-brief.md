Polymarket and Kalshi political markets are systematically miscalibrated at long horizons: prices compressed toward 50%, persistently understating favorites' true probability. A Feb 2026 decomposition study (Le, arXiv:2602.19520) attributes 87.3% of cross-market calibration variance to four stable components, with politics the dominant miscalibrated domain — Polymarket politics mean slope 1.31 vs. sports 1.08, crypto 1.05.

Thesis: that slope will not flatten to within ±0.05 of 1.0 across the 2026 cycle, because the bias is structural — bounded liquidity, capital cost, behavioral 50%-compression — not informational error that arbitrage can close.

Why it matters for swarm-fund: direct academic justification for CalibrationGap/Revenant's premise; if Revenant is under-indexed on long-horizon politics, it's leaving edge on the table.
