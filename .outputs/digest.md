*prediction markets — 2026-05-06*

_TL;DR: Blockchain.com ships 30-second BTC binary contracts as a new 'skill-based' product, a federal judge permanently blocks Arizona from prosecuting Kalshi on preemption grounds, and Kalshi's April taker volume overtakes Polymarket for the first time at $5.42B vs $1.99B._

1. *Blockchain.com launches SnapMarkets — 30-second BTC binary contracts from $1*
Users wager whether bitcoin's price rises or falls in 30-second windows; live chat, streak tracking, global leaderboard; non-custodial wallet by default. Not available in the US or UK. Marketed as 'skill-based' — structurally a binary option, the class banned for retail in the EU/UK.
Why it matters: Second venue selling sub-minute BTC contracts after Kalshi KXBTC. More fast-resolution venues mean more cross-venue convergence data and more candidate legs for short-window arb.
https://www.coindesk.com/business/2026/05/06/blockchain-com-launches-snapmarkets-as-prediction-markets-move-into-faster-crypto-bets

2. *Federal judge permanently blocks Arizona's criminal case against Kalshi*
Judge Liburdi made permanent the April TRO, ruling federal law preempts state gambling statutes for CFTC-registered exchanges. Arizona AG Kris Mayes is barred from prosecuting the 20 misdemeanor counts; four of those targeted Kalshi's 2026/2028 election contracts. CFTC Chair Mike Selig: 'the CFTC has full jurisdiction over prediction markets.'
Why it matters: First district-level permanent ruling on prediction-market preemption — sets the precedent every other state suit (Connecticut, Illinois, Nevada, NJ) will be measured against.
https://defirate.com/news/federal-judge-permanently-blocks-arizona-criminal-case-vs-kalshi/

3. *Kalshi overtakes Polymarket on April monthly volume — $5.42B vs $1.99B taker*
Sector taker volume hit $8.6B; in notional terms, Kalshi posted $14.8B vs Polymarket's $9B (plus $1.26B from Polymarket US). Polymarket still led on unique users — 678,342 vs Kalshi's roughly 80,000 — putting Kalshi's per-user volume near 8x Polymarket's. Sports drove most of the Kalshi lead.
Why it matters: First month Kalshi has outrun Polymarket on monthly volume, compounding with the Arizona ruling. Liquidity weight is shifting to the regulated venue; convergence-trade fills get easier on the Kalshi side, thinner on Polymarket outside political and India-election books.
